Section 02

Specific Market Risks

KOLOMIETSSA's educational content and analytical materials discuss various financial markets. Each market carries unique and significant risks:

Equity Markets (Stocks)

Equity securities are subject to market risk, liquidity risk, company-specific risk, sector risk, and macroeconomic risk. Stock prices can experience rapid and dramatic declines due to earnings disappointments, regulatory action, broader market sell-offs, geopolitical events, or changes in investor sentiment. Companies can and do go bankrupt, resulting in total loss of invested equity.

Derivatives (Options, Futures, CFDs)

Derivative instruments are highly complex and carry substantially higher risks than direct equity investments. Derivatives may be subject to leverage, expiry dates, counterparty risk, and liquidity risk. CFDs (Contracts for Difference) are leveraged products that can result in losses exceeding your initial deposit. The majority of retail investors lose money when trading CFDs.

Cryptocurrency Assets

Cryptocurrency markets are characterised by extreme volatility, limited regulatory oversight, security risks associated with exchanges and wallets, and the potential for complete loss of value. Crypto assets are not covered by any deposit protection scheme. Regulatory changes can have sudden and severe impact on crypto asset valuations.

Foreign Exchange (Forex)

Currency trading involves significant leverage and the foreign exchange market is highly volatile. Exchange rates are affected by interest rate differentials, political events, economic data releases, and central bank policy — many of which are unpredictable. Leverage in forex trading amplifies both gains and losses.

Section 03

Leverage & Margin Risk

Many of the trading strategies and instruments discussed in KOLOMIETSSA educational materials involve the use of leverage. Leverage allows traders to control a larger position than their deposited capital would otherwise permit.

Leverage is a double-edged instrument. While it can amplify profits, it equally amplifies losses. A 10:1 leveraged position can result in total loss of deposited margin from a 10% adverse price movement. Higher leverage ratios increase this risk proportionally.

Margin calls may require you to deposit additional funds at short notice or your broker may automatically close your position at a loss. You should ensure you understand how margin requirements work at your specific broker before using any leveraged strategy.

KOLOMIETSSA's educational content discussing leveraged strategies does not constitute a recommendation to use leverage. Decisions regarding leverage should be made solely by the trader based on their own risk assessment, experience, and financial resilience.

Section 05

Past Performance Disclaimer

Past performance is not a reliable indicator of future results. Historical performance data presented anywhere on this Platform — including backtest results, live track records, portfolio performance statistics, or testimonials — does not and cannot predict future performance with any degree of reliability.

Markets are dynamic and non-stationary systems. A strategy that performed well in historical market conditions may perform poorly or catastrophically in different market environments due to regime changes, increased volatility, reduced liquidity, structural shifts in market microstructure, or correlation breakdowns.

Backtest Limitations

Backtested results are subject to significant limitations including but not limited to: look-ahead bias, overfitting to historical data, failure to account for transaction costs and slippage, survivorship bias in instrument selection, and the fundamental impossibility of replicating historical conditions in real-time trading environments.

Hypothetical Performance

Any hypothetical or simulated performance results shown on the Platform have inherent limitations. Hypothetical results do not reflect actual trading and may not account for market impact, liquidity constraints, or the psychological pressures of live trading. Actual results could differ materially and adversely from hypothetical presentations.
